Output File - Flow Control and Error Control Protocols Simulation
===========================================================


1. Simulation Environment:
   - Topology: PC0 (Sender) -- Switch -- PC1 (Receiver)
   - All simulations performed in Cisco Packet Tracer Simulation Mode
   - ICMP PDUs used to simulate frames
   - Errors simulated by manually deleting PDUs in Event List

2. Performance Results:

Stop-and-Wait ARQ:
------------------
- No Error: Very low throughput, only 1 frame in flight at a time
- With Error (10%): Frequent timeouts and retransmissions of single frame
- Efficiency: Lowest among all three protocols
- Observation: Simple implementation but highly inefficient due to constant waiting for ACK

Go-Back-N ARQ:
--------------
- No Error: Good utilization, multiple frames sent before ACK
- With Error (10%): Multiple good frames retransmitted after error
- Efficiency: Better than Stop-and-Wait when error-free, but drops significantly with errors
- Observation: Sender goes back to the erroneous frame and retransmits all subsequent frames

Selective Repeat ARQ:
---------------------
- No Error: Highest utilization with multiple frames in window
- With Error (10%): Only the lost frame is retransmitted
- Efficiency: Highest among all three, especially under error conditions
- Observation: Receiver buffers out-of-order good frames (conceptually observed)

3. Comparative Analysis:
   - Stop-and-Wait: Simplest but poorest performance
   - Go-Back-N: Balanced but wastes bandwidth on errors
   - Selective Repeat: Most efficient in error-prone networks

4. Impact of Network Conditions:
   - Low error rate     → Go-Back-N and Selective Repeat perform similarly well
   - High error rate    → Selective Repeat performs significantly better
   - High delay         → Stop-and-Wait suffers the most

Overall Conclusion:
Selective Repeat ARQ offers the best throughput and efficiency under varying network conditions,
followed by Go-Back-N ARQ, while Stop-and-Wait ARQ is the least efficient but easiest to implement.
